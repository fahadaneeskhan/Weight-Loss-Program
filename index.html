RUNNING PLAN
10K comeback · Knee-smart progression · 5 phases
Context: You ran a 10K a year ago so your aerobic base isn't zero — but at 115 kg, your joints need a re-entry period. This plan gets you back to 10K in ~4 months. Running happens on Wed + Sat, replacing your walks those days.
PHASE 1 Re-entry Weeks 1–2 ▾
📍 ~3.5 km📅 Weeks 1–2
Run 2 min / Walk 2 min × 8 rounds
Your lungs will be ready before your joints. Don't push pace. This phase is about re-conditioning tendons and knees at 115 kg body weight.
PHASE 2 Building Base Weeks 3–4 ▾
📍 ~4 km📅 Weeks 3–4
Run 3 min / Walk 1 min × 7 rounds
Pace should be conversational — if you can't hold a sentence, slow down. No heroics at this stage.
PHASE 3 Continuous Run Weeks 5–7 ▾
📍 ~4–4.5 km📅 Weeks 5–7
20–25 min continuous easy run
First time running without walk breaks in over a year. Keep it very easy. This is a milestone — don't celebrate it by going faster.
PHASE 4 5K Target Weeks 8–11 ▾
📍 ~5 km📅 Weeks 8–11
30 min continuous, 3× per week
By now you'll be ~108 kg — significantly easier on your knees. Add a third run session on Sunday (easy jog). Park run on a Saturday is a great milestone to target.
PHASE 5 Back to 10K Month 4+ ▾
📍 Build to 8–10 km📅 Month 4+
Long run Saturday · Two shorter runs midweek
At ~100 kg your old 10K form comes back fast. Register for a race — giving yourself a date is one of the most powerful motivation tools available. You've done a 10K before; you'll do it again, lighter.
Running Rules for the Knee
👟
Shoes first
If your running shoes are over 18 months old, replace them. At 115 kg, cushioning compresses faster. This is the single most important injury-prevention step.
🌡️
Warm up every time
5 min brisk walk, then leg swings, calf raises, and quad stretches before you run. Cold muscles at heavier body weight = recipe for the knee tightening up.
🐢
Run slower than feels natural
Your 10K muscle memory will push you to run at old pace. Resist it. Slow enough to hold a conversation — that's Zone 2 and it's where fat burning and endurance are built.
📉
10% rule
Never increase weekly distance by more than 10% from the previous week. Connective tissue adapts slower than cardio fitness — this is how you avoid shin splints and knee injuries.
🛑
Pain vs tightness
Tightness = expected, warm up and continue. Sharp or stabbing pain = stop immediately. A skipped session is nothing compared to a 6-week injury.
⚠️ After a hard Day 2 leg session on Tuesday, keep Wednesday's run genuinely easy. Don't stack a hard leg session with a hard run the next morning.
